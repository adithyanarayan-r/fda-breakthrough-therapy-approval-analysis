# FDA Breakthrough Therapy Approval Analysis (2020–2025)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes FDA Breakthrough Therapy drug approvals between 2020 and 2025 using Power BI.  
The dashboard examines therapy area distribution, annual approval trends, and leading pharmaceutical companies.

## Objective
To identify trends in breakthrough drug approvals and highlight dominant therapy areas and leading pharmaceutical contributors.

---

## Key Insights
### Overall Summary
- Total approvals (2020–2025): **181**
- Total companies: **101**
- Total therapy areas: **12**

---

### Approval Trend

- Breakthrough therapy approvals gradually declined between 2020 and 2023 before recovering again in 2024 and 2025.  
- The slowdown may partly reflect delays in clinical trials and regulatory processes during the COVID period, but it also shows how approval activity depends heavily on when late-stage pipelines mature.  
- The recovery after 2023 suggests that development activity and regulatory momentum started stabilizing again across the industry.


---

### Therapy Area Insights

- Oncology contributed nearly 65% of all breakthrough therapy approvals between 2020–2025, far higher than any other therapy area.  
- This likely reflects the strong industry focus on cancer therapeutics, where there is high unmet medical need along with significant investment in targeted and precision-based treatments.  
- Oncology programs may also benefit from faster regulatory attention and biomarker-driven clinical trials, which can increase the chances of receiving breakthrough designation.  
- In comparison, non-oncology therapy areas showed more scattered approval patterns, suggesting that innovation activity outside oncology was more distributed across different segments.

---

### Company Insights

- Genentech, Daiichi Sankyo, and Novartis were among the leading contributors to breakthrough therapy approvals during the study period.  
- At the same time, no single company maintained dominance every year, showing that breakthrough innovation is still highly dependent on pipeline timing and clinical success.  
- The data suggests that companies with strong late-stage oncology pipelines were better positioned to secure repeated breakthrough designations.  
- It also highlights how regulatory execution and focused R&D strategy can influence competitive positioning within the pharmaceutical industry.

---

### Year-wise Behavior

- Oncology approvals remained consistently strong throughout the entire study period, even during years when overall approval activity declined.  
- This consistency suggests that oncology continued to receive sustained industry investment and regulatory focus compared to other therapy areas.  
- In contrast, non-oncology categories showed greater year-to-year fluctuation, indicating that innovation activity outside oncology was comparatively less stable and more dependent on individual pipeline successes.

---

### Competitive Dynamics

- No single company consistently led breakthrough therapy approvals across all years.  
- Leadership shifted over time as different companies advanced late-stage pipelines and achieved clinical milestones.  
- This suggests that competitive advantage in breakthrough innovation is closely tied to pipeline maturity, regulatory execution, and the timing of clinical success rather than company size alone.  
- The pattern also reflects a highly active and evolving innovation landscape within the pharmaceutical industry.

---

### Advanced Insight

- The decline in approvals between 2020 and 2023 likely reflects the broader impact of the COVID-19 period on clinical trial timelines, patient recruitment, and regulatory workflows.  
- However, the trend also highlights how breakthrough designation activity can fluctuate based on the timing and maturity of late-stage development pipelines. 
- The recovery observed after 2023 suggests a gradual normalization of development activity along with renewed momentum in high-priority therapeutic programs.

## Key Visualizations

- **Total Breakthrough Approvals KPI**  
  Provides an overview of total approvals, companies, and therapy areas.

- **Annual Approval Trend (Line Chart)**  
  Shows year-wise variation in approvals, highlighting the dip from 2020–2023 and recovery post-2023.

- **Therapy Area Distribution (Donut Chart)**  
  Displays the share of approvals by therapy area, with oncology dominating.

- **Approvals by Year & Therapy Area (Stacked Bar Chart)**  
  Illustrates how different therapy areas contribute to approvals across years.

- **Total Approvals by Company (Bar Chart)**  
  Identifies leading pharmaceutical companies based on approval counts.

- **Top 3 Companies in Oncology (Line Chart)**  
  Compares year-wise performance of top companies in oncology approvals.
